Audio samples from "MultiLingual Audio Deepfake Detection (MADD)"

Abstract: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) come up with advances in generative AI. Which is capable of generating deepfake data (such as audio, images, and video). Deepfakes are not replicas of the input data but are perceptually unnoticeable from genuine data. It raises significant concerns about the integrity and authenticity of digital content. Nowadays, audio deepfake detection become complex due to the various neural network architectures, such as Autoregressive (AR), Non-Autoregressive (NAR), Flow-based, GANs-based, and Diffusion-based models. Each of these architectures introduces unique contributions and related limitations. Our research is pioneering in its approach to multilingual deepfake detection, addressing significant gaps in existing work. Currently, deepfake detection is predominantly focused on English and a few other major languages (on a lighter note, creates Language Bias), which limits its generalization in languages with different accents and linguistic structures. The second novel aspect of our work lies in integrating multilingual deepfake generation as an important part of the deepfake detection process. Currently, deepfake detection research is often limited to feature extraction and analysis, including Mel spectrograms, MFCCs, pitch (or fundamental frequency $F_{0}$), prosodic features, classifier model selection, training, evaluation, and benchmarking. However, the deepfake generation process itself remains largely unexplored and is treated as a 'black box'. This lack of understanding hampers the development of effective detection mechanisms. The goal is to understand how deepfakes are generated to closely mimic genuine signals and inversely develop a robust multilingual detection model. Our proposed work follows responsible AI principles from the development to the deployment process such as open documentation. Diverse dataset, continuous improvement while ensuring transparency, user privacy, and avoiding the bias in detection algorithms, to promote responsible AI practices.
